# Customer Journey Map
## Overview
The customer journey map for the payment-orchestrator product outlines the phases a financial institution goes through from initial awareness to expansion of the platform. 

## Phases
### Aware
* **Trigger Event**: Inefficient payment operations and transaction monitoring processes lead to increased costs and decreased client satisfaction.
* **Friction Points**: Manual payment tracking, lack of real-time transaction monitoring, and inadequate communication with clients.
* **User Emotions**: Frustration, concern about reputation, and anxiety about potential losses.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Introduction to the payment-orchestrator platform through targeted marketing campaigns, highlighting its benefits in streamlining payment operations and improving client communication.
* **Metric**: Website traffic and social media engagement (target: 500 unique visitors, 100 social media interactions within the first 6 weeks).

### Consider
* **Trigger Event**: Identification of the need for a more efficient payment operations system, potentially sparked by the awareness phase.
* **Friction Points**: Evaluating the cost-benefit analysis of implementing a new platform, concerns about integration with existing systems, and assessing the competition.
* **User Emotions**: Curiosity, skepticism, and cautious optimism.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Offering demos, free trials, or pilot projects to showcase the platform's capabilities, ease of integration, and return on investment.
* **Metric**: Number of demo requests and trial sign-ups (target: 20 demo requests, 10 trial sign-ups within the first 3 months).

### Try
* **Trigger Event**: Decision to trial or pilot the payment-orchestrator platform based on the evaluation in the consider phase.
* **Friction Points**: Technical difficulties during onboarding, initial learning curve for users, and potential data migration issues.
* **User Emotions**: Anticipation, potential frustration if expectations are not met, and relief if the platform performs as promised.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Providing comprehensive onboarding support, responsive customer service, and ensuring seamless data migration.
* **Metric**: Trial to paid conversion rate (target: 75% conversion rate within the first 6 months).

### Adopt
* **Trigger Event**: Successful trial or pilot leading to the decision to adopt the payment-orchestrator platform fully.
* **Friction Points**: Full-scale implementation challenges, training all relevant staff, and ensuring ongoing support.
* **User Emotions**: Satisfaction, relief, and optimism about future operational efficiencies and client satisfaction improvements.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Offering premium support packages, regular software updates with new features, and success stories from similar institutions.
* **Metric**: Customer retention rate (target: 90% retention rate after the first year).

### Expand
* **Trigger Event**: Realization of the benefits and potential for further optimization and expansion of the payment-orchestrator platform within the institution.
* **Friction Points**: Identifying new areas for integration or expansion, justifying additional investment, and managing change within the organization.
* **User Emotions**: Confidence, ambition, and a desire for continued improvement.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Developing strategic partnerships, offering customized solutions, and providing insights into future product developments.
* **Metric**: Upsell and cross-sell revenue growth (target: 20% annual growth in upsell and cross-sell revenue).

By understanding and addressing the needs, emotions, and friction points at each phase of the customer journey, the payment-orchestrator platform can effectively guide financial institutions from awareness to expansion, ultimately delivering a superior customer experience and driving business growth.
